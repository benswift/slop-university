Slop University's newest bus-stop and campus-screen campaign puts a face,
rather than a finding, at the centre of the pitch.

Unveiled today under the line "What The Course Leaves Behind," the artwork
casts Dr Ingeborg Nwachukwu, Lecturer and Convenor of the School of
Continuous Improvement's Capability Sprints program, at an ordinary desk
some months after a course badge would have been filed away. It runs across
the University's bus-stop panels and campus digital screens in matched
daylight and evening renderings built to hold their legibility after dark.

> A course can teach you anything it likes, in a room built for the
> purpose. What I study is which of it is still there the first ordinary
> Tuesday back at your own desk.
>
> --- Dr Ingeborg Nwachukwu, Lecturer and Convenor, Capability Sprints

The casting is not incidental. Dr Nwachukwu's own research follows
Capability Sprint cohorts past graduation, tracking which habits outlast
the certificate and which quietly lapse once the desk returns to normal;
the School frames her presence in the campaign as a small extension of
that same instrument, turning the recruitment pitch into one more site its
own measurement now reaches.

"A campaign that couldn't survive its own scrutiny wouldn't get very far
past our door," said Dr Bram Ntuli, Convenor of Improvement Grand Rounds.
"This one, we're told, held up."

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.qxn1nv.
