The Master of Applied Measurement has never pretended prospective students
arrive without an instinct already sharpened at home. Its newest recruitment
campaign points them toward where that instinct already lives: the basket by
the front door where a household's phones go to be forgotten for the
evening.

Unveiled today under the line "You Already Notice Who Reaches Back In," the
artwork sets a woven basket of phones on a hallway table, a hand mid-reach
past a row of coats on hooks, lamp light pooling across the floor. It runs
across the University's bus-stop panels and campus digital screens in
matched daylight and evening renderings built to hold their legibility after
dark.

> A basket by the door doesn't stop anyone reaching in. It just makes the
> reaching countable.
>
> --- Associate Professor Casimir Beng, Lead of the Adaptive Metrics Lab

The School of Continuous Improvement frames the campaign as recruitment
pared to its instrument: the Master of Applied Measurement spends its first
semester turning a household habit prospective students already keep score
of, informally, into a method with a citation.

"Most of what we teach, someone in the house has already been doing since
before the degree existed," said Dr Renke Sabel, Convenor of the Indicator
Commons. "We just ask them to write the method section."

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.t0neif.
