A café blackboard only tells the truth for as long as no one has picked up
the chalk. Slop University's newest campaign turns that narrow window into a
ranking.

The artwork, unveiled today across the University's bus-stop panels and
campus digital screens, sets a specials board mid-rewrite --- one hand
rubbing out a line the other hasn't finished chalking --- under the line
"First To Flag The Swap." The tall portrait format is built for the
University's signage network, with a matching evening rendering for panels
running after dark.

> We used to think the interesting question was how a plan drifts from what's
> written. The interesting question turned out to be what a specials board
> can teach a five-year strategy about drifting in front of an audience,
> instead of behind one.
>
> --- Dr Anneke Tolan, Senior Research Fellow, School of Emergent Priorities

The claim is real in the sense that the University controls every part of
it: the Horizon Register composite behind the ranking is the School of
Emergent Priorities' own instrument, and the University is the only body it
has ever measured. Six quarters running, the composite has placed Slop
University first for the gap between a specials board changing and a table
noticing --- a placement the School treats less as competition than as a
standing self-audit with a leaderboard of one.

"Grading your own homework is usually a complaint," said Dr Dagny Okafor,
Convenor of Evaluation of Evaluation. "Grading it in public, on a bus
shelter, is at least honest about the arrangement."
