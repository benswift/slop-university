The University's only escalator has stopped, on average, more than once a
fortnight for three years, and each time Facilities' service log records the
same tidy outcome: reported within fifteen minutes, every time. A new paper
from the School of Continuous Improvement asks who is actually doing the
reporting, and finds the honest answer is almost nobody the University would
recognise.

Lecturer Dagny Okafor, Lecturer Bram Ntuli, and Senior Lecturer Renke Sabel
spoke with forty-eight people near the escalator --- students, general
staff, and every person who has held the University's floor-marshal
appointment since it was quietly extended to cover escalator reporting
three years ago --- asking a simple question: whose job do they think this
is?

> A duty can be perfectly assigned and still have no one standing under it.
> That's not a contradiction; it's just what three unrevised years does to
> an org chart.
>
> --- Dr Dagny Okafor, Lecturer and Convenor, Evaluation of Evaluation

"It's not that the escalator goes unreported," said Lecturer Bram Ntuli, a
co-author. "It's that almost everyone we spoke to, including three of our
own floor marshals, was surprised to hear it was ever their job in the
first place."

Facilities says the finding raises exactly the kind of question its termly
appointment cycle should be able to answer, and is reviewing how the
floor-marshal role is communicated to the people who hold it.

The full paper is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.hfz29u.
