A finding does not normally leave the research repository until a reader
goes looking for it. The School of Emergent Priorities' newest campaign
moves one out onto the street instead.

The artwork, going up today across the University's bus-stop panels and
campus digital screens, depicts a share-house kitchen bench near midnight
--- a phone lit among a stack of abandoned mugs --- under the line "The Chat
That Won't Stop Talking." A companion evening rendering keeps the same
image legible on panels running after dark.

> Most of what we publish stays inside the repository. This one got a
> second life on a bus shelter, which is more attention than a group chat
> usually earns for outliving its own point.
>
> --- Senior Lecturer and Convenor Dr Runa Adegoke, School of Emergent
> Priorities

The campaign draws directly on Dr Adegoke's own study, which tracked 64
share-house group chats for fourteen months and found that a self-written
end date bought a household months of relative quiet it would otherwise
have gone without. The School regards the billboard less as marketing than
as the finding completing its intended circuit --- back out into the kind
of household that produced it.

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.xr441g.
