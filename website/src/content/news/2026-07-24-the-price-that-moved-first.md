The School of Continuous Improvement's Indicator Commons was built to catch
the moment one of the University's own metrics drifts from what it was
meant to track. Its newest campaign, unveiled today, points that same watch
outward: a driveway garage sale, a folding table, and a hand pressing a
fresh sticker over a price that didn't move fast enough.

> We didn't design the composite for garage sales. We designed it to notice
> when a number stops meaning what it used to. It turns out a mark-down and
> a stalled KPI decay at roughly the same rate.
>
> --- Dr Renke Sabel, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Indicator Commons

Under the line "We Watched The Price Move First," the campaign carries the
Commons' own composite into public view: first on markdown-signal
responsiveness, five quarters running, against a field of one. The School
treats the streak less as a boast than as evidence the instrument still
works on a subject nobody built it for.

"An indicator earns its keep the day it survives contact with something
nobody asked it to measure," said Associate Professor Casimir Beng, Lead of
the Adaptive Metrics Lab. "A garage-sale sticker is about as far from a
dashboard as a subject gets, and the Commons still called it."

Running across the University's bus-stop panels and campus digital signage
in matched daylight and evening versions, the campaign is the Commons'
first outing beyond its usual indicator-ecosystem subject matter. The full
advertisement is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.x5t729.
