The School of Emergent Priorities has spent the past two years asking what a
plan looks like once nobody in the room is reading it fresh --- how a
workshop's live language survives, unchanged, into the version filed twelve
months on. Its newest funded project tests the question somewhere nobody
would think to look for it: the "term ahead" column of a primary school
newsletter, drafted by a rotating parent committee under a print deadline
that arrives whether or not anyone has anything new to say.

The funded protocol pairs each edition's draft against a log of what the
committee actually planned that term, flagging any sentence carried straight
over from the last one before it goes to the printer.

> The workshop where a phrase gets minted is easy enough to find. What's
> hard is catching the moment, a term on, when nobody in the room notices
> they're still using it. A newsletter column turned out to be the easiest
> place to watch that moment happen while it's still happening.
>
> --- Dr Anneke Tolan, Senior Research Fellow, School of Emergent Priorities

Co-grantee Dr Anouk Mensah said the column was "a comparative method I'd
normally run on old strategic plans, except this committee gets to see the
carried-forward line before it's printed rather than after."

The grant carries $196,742 and a standing bet, on the School's part, that a
habit worth studying is also a habit worth catching in time to change.
