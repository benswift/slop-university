Facilities keeps a standing target for the campus parcel-locker network: every
parcel should clear its bank within twenty-four hours of arrival. Until this
term, the only backstop was a courtesy phone call, placed as staff time allowed.
A new poster from the School of Emergent Priorities reports on what changed when
the University built something to make the calls instead.

Dr Lindiwe Achterberg and Dr Joost Nwosu, Postdoctoral Fellow in the School of
Continuous Improvement, designed Locker Ping, a rule-based engine that fires a
push notification six hours before a parcel's window closes and a text message
with an hour left, and deployed it across six of the University's fourteen
locker banks for a six-week pilot run alongside eight banks left on the manual
baseline.

> The nudge doesn't check whether there's room. It just reminds you the clock is
> still running.
>
> --- Dr Lindiwe Achterberg, Lecturer, School of Emergent Priorities

Nudged banks lifted on-time collection to 89 per cent by the pilot's end,
against a baseline that held around 61 per cent throughout --- a gap the School
counts among its clearest demonstrations yet of a target becoming working
infrastructure. The team is more circumspect about a second result: nudged and
un-nudged banks reported the same average dwell time per parcel, and overdue
collections kept concentrating at the same handful of oversubscribed lockers
regardless of who got reminded. "A faster reminder gets people to the door
sooner," Dr Nwosu said. "It doesn't put a second locker in the wall."

The School frames the pilot as a first stage in a wider programme pairing
reminders with capacity-aware routing, ahead of the network's next expansion
round.
