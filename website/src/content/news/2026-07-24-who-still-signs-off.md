The School of Continuous Improvement has spent several years cataloguing
which of an institution's smallest instruments still mean what they once
did. Its newest recruitment campaign puts one directly in the shot: the
laminated sign-off sticker fixed to the tea-room microwave, present on the
door for longer than anyone currently on staff can account for.

Unveiled today ahead of this spring's Demo Quarter, the artwork sets the
microwave beneath a crowded noticeboard, its sticker still catching the
light, under the line "The Signature Retired. The Sticker Didn't." It runs
across the University's bus-stop panels and campus digital screens in
matched daylight and evening renderings built to hold their legibility
after dark.

> Auditing what a credential still verifies rarely tells you much about the
> credential. It tells you who stopped checking, and when.
>
> --- Dr Dagny Okafor, Lecturer and Convenor, Evaluation of Evaluation

Demo Quarter, the School's quarterly Masters-program showcase, exists to
put a project in front of an audience under exactly the kind of scrutiny a
laminated sticker never gets a second time. The campaign borrows the same
standard for its own casting call: an object everyone recognises, still
trading on an approval nobody currently present actually gave.

"A showcase slot is supposed to be earned every quarter," said Dr Thandiwe
Solberg, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Demo Quarter. "The microwave's
sticker did it once, in a year nobody's checked, and has been coasting on
the paperwork ever since."
