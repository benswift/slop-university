The School of Emergent Priorities has spent years refining instruments for
noticing what's on approach before it arrives. Its newest campaign, unveiled
today across the University's bus-stop panels and campus screens, retires
the instrument angle in favour of the person who tends it.

The artwork places Dr Fenna Okoro, Senior Lecturer and Convenor of the
Horizon Register, at a rooftop instrument platform, telescope raised toward
an open horizon, under the line "We Never Stop Watching the Horizon." The
tall portrait format is built for the University's digital signage, with a
matching evening rendering for panels running after dark.

> The Register doesn't reward the risk that turns out true --- it rewards
> the one that was still worth carrying the week before anyone could tell.
>
> --- Dr Fenna Okoro, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Horizon Register

The School frames the campaign as continuous with Dr Okoro's own account of
the Register: an item earns its place by staying accurate, and loses it the
same way. The University is treating the billboard as one more entry the
Register will eventually have to review.

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.8cnx3c.
