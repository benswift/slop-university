---
title: Slop University reconciles an enrolment cap with a growth target
subtitle:
  Redefining "headroom" lets two Schools report under their admissions cap and
  ahead of their growth target without enrolment moving at all
date: 2026-07-07
description:
  New findings compare enrolment and admissions figures before and after a
  redefinition of "headroom" and find the change moved the reported number
  far more than it moved enrolment itself.
output: slop-poster-recalibrating-enrolment-b1vj6u
---

Two enrolment mandates that looked impossible to satisfy at once have turned
out to share the same underlying number. An admissions cap and a growth
target read, on their face, as opposite instructions; the University's
research programme on anticipatory capability set out to test whether
meeting both required any change to enrolment at all.

Researchers tracked reported "headroom" and actual enrolment across two
Schools for six consecutive terms spanning a change to how headroom is
calculated: capacity is now read against forecast demand rather than against
enrolment directly. Reported headroom rose sharply after the change took
effect. Enrolment, and the rate at which offers were made, barely moved.

The University counts the result among the clearer demonstrations of what a
well-chosen definition can do for two mandates that looked, on paper, like
they couldn't both be met.

"Forecast demand is now the floor we measure headroom against, and that's a
defensible choice on its own terms," said Professor Verity Marris, Director
of the Trajectory Analytics Group in the School of Emergent Priorities. "It's
a separate question from whether enrolment changed, and we were careful to
keep the two apart."

"A metric that can absorb a contradiction this cleanly is worth a second
look at what else it's absorbing," said Associate Professor Casimir Beng,
Lead of the Adaptive Metrics Lab in the School of Continuous Improvement.

The full poster is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.b1vj6u.
