> Most people at a bus stop have already overruled the countdown sign by
> the time it changes. We only teach the habit on purpose.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director, Trajectory Analytics Group

A new Slop University advertising campaign for the Master of Priority
Studies makes that overruling its whole pitch. Unveiled today across the
University's bus-stop panels and campus digital screens, the artwork places
a commuter at a shelter, gaze fixed on the road rather than the sign beside
them, under the line "You Knew Before The Sign Did." It runs in matched
daylight and evening renderings built to hold their legibility on panels
after dark.

The School of Emergent Priorities frames the campaign as recruitment in the
loosest sense: less an invitation to learn something unfamiliar than an
offer to formalise an instinct a prospective student already carries into
every school run, supermarket queue, and late tram. The Master of Priority
Studies, the School says, spends its first semester turning that private
hunch into a method with a name, a literature, and a mark.

"A gut feeling is just a finding that hasn't been written up yet," said Dr
Ingrid Vasseur, Convenor of Futures in Committee. "We ask students to write
it up."

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.4kxqdu.
