> An instrument doesn't fail the day it's forgotten. It fails a little more
> every day after.
>
> --- Dr Marit Osayande, Lecturer and Convenor, Living Dashboard

That's how Dr Osayande describes the gap her research keeps finding, and
from today it is also the line fronting Slop University's newest
recruitment campaign. Unveiled across the University's bus-stop panels and
campus digital screens under "Nobody's Checked The Box In Years," the
artwork sets Dr Osayande beside a roadside honesty-box stall gone quietly
unattended --- a cobwebbed corner, a blank ledger page, its tally still open
to whoever passes. It runs in matched daylight and evening renderings built
to hold their legibility after dark.

The School of Continuous Improvement treats the casting as closer to an
admission than a metaphor. Dr Osayande convenes the Living Dashboard, the
School's own always-on self-measurement wall, and her research spends most
of its attention on the upkeep a self-measurement instrument stops
receiving once its novelty passes. A roadside honesty box, the School
notes, keeps the same kind of ledger on a considerably smaller budget.

"We spend a lot of time asking what a dashboard is quietly deciding for
us," said Dr Mirela Hanke, Postdoctoral Fellow. "It's nice to see the
question asked somewhere with fewer screens."
