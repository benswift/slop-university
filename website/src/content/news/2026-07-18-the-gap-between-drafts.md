Futures in Committee, the School of Emergent Priorities' standing seminar
series, opens its next session on a register most of the University assumes
runs itself. Dr Fenna Okoro, who convenes the Horizon Register, will walk the
series through what actually happens before an item earns a number, and what
has to happen before one is finally allowed to lose it.

> An item doesn't arrive on the register because someone decided it mattered.
> It arrives because someone drafted it in a form the register would accept,
> and that's a completely different achievement.
>
> --- Dr Fenna Okoro, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Horizon Register

The session extends Dr Okoro's own work tracing an entry's edit history, this
time turned toward the committee moment itself: the wording that gets an item
over the admission bar, and the quieter wording that eventually gets it struck
off without anyone calling it a retirement. Dr Ingrid Vasseur, who convenes
Futures in Committee, said the series exists to make exactly that moment
visible. "Most of what a committee does to a horizon item happens in the gap
between drafts," she said, "and that gap is where the series has always
wanted to spend its time."

Futures in Committee runs through the teaching period; the session's time and
venue are to follow.
