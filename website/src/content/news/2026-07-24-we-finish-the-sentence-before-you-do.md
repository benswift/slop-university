Futures in Committee, the School of Emergent Priorities' seminar series on
how foresight talk becomes committee business, has a new campaign making
the case for turning up before the case gets made without you.

> The room likes to think it's still deciding. Half the time it's watching
> the sentence it already started finish itself.
>
> --- Dr Ingrid Vasseur, Lecturer and Convenor, Futures in Committee

Unveiled today under the line "We Finish The Sentence Before You Do," the
artwork sets a phone mid-reply on a crowded meeting table, its screen
answering itself before anyone has spoken, while behind it a motion sheet
on an easel already carries a full page of wording no one in the room has
proposed yet. It runs on the University's bus-stop panels and campus
screens, with a paired night rendering for after dark.

"A demonstration slot and a committee minute are cut from the same cloth,"
said Dr Thandiwe Solberg, Senior Lecturer and Convenor of Demo Quarter.
"Both get finished well before the audience arrives to watch them happen."

Futures in Committee holds sessions across the current teaching period. The
full advertisement is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.ldoad5.
