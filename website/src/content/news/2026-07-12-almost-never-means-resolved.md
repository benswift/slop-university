> Something that never gets a decision doesn't vanish. It just finds a
> quieter file to live in.
>
> --- Lecturer Marek Solheim, School of Emergent Priorities

Slop University's exit sign-out table marked more than six in ten
bathroom-break flags "resolved" in every term studied. A new paper from the
School of Emergent Priorities and the School of Continuous Improvement
finds that only 13.5% of those flags were ever referred through the
University's academic-integrity process and closed against an actual
decision.

Two coders spent months working through eight terms of the University's
own archived exam-hall paperwork, sorting all 617 flagged sign-outs into
one of four plain categories --- formally resolved, quietly reclassified
onto another student's file, administratively closed, or simply left open
once the exam period ended. A second pass, coded with the sign-out table's
checkbox hidden from view, returned almost the same picture.

"The strength of a checkbox is that everyone reads it the same way," said
Senior Lecturer Renke Sabel of the School of Continuous Improvement, "which
turns out to be its whole limitation once someone actually checks what
sits behind it."

The University reads the result as consistent with its habit of holding
its own administrative shortcuts to the standard of evidence it expects
everywhere else, and the paper's authors say the four-category taxonomy is
now available to any unit willing to look behind its own checkbox before
the next exam period closes.

The full paper is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.jw4nw9.
