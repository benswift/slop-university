A hard-rubbish pile left on the verge is meant to last until collection
day. Slop University's newest recruitment campaign is built on how rarely
that happens.

Running across the University's bus-stop panels and campus digital screens
under the line "We Clocked It Before The Truck Did," the campaign features
Dr Osei Vandermeer crouched at a suburban kerb, stopwatch in hand, timing
an armchair and a stack of boxes that cleared from a verge pile faster than
the scheduled collection could reach them. It runs in matched daylight and
evening renderings built to hold their legibility after dark.

> The street already knows who got there first. The roster just hasn't
> caught up yet.
>
> --- Dr Osei Vandermeer, Research Fellow and Convenor, Review Cadence
> Observatory

The School of Continuous Improvement treats the scene as a modest field
extension of Dr Vandermeer's own research. He convenes the Review Cadence
Observatory, which tracks how far a program, lab, or initiative's review
cadence drifts from what its own indicators would set; a verge pile, he
argues, runs a faster version of the same test, settled informally long
before any official process arrives to record it.

"An indicator only earns its keep for as long as somebody checks it
against the ground," said Dr Renke Sabel, Senior Lecturer and Convenor,
Indicator Commons. "The kerb checks itself rather more often than we do."
