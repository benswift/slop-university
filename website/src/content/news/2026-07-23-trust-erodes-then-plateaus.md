> Trust doesn't have a floor here. It has a very stubborn plateau.
>
> --- Senior Lecturer and Convenor Dr Runa Adegoke, School of Emergent
> Priorities

A high school's tap-in gate scanner turns a child's arrival into one
scanned event, forwarded to the front office as the day's record. A new
Slop University research poster asks whether parents treat that tap as
more authoritative than what they already know about the school run, and
surveyed 163 parents across five outer-suburban schools to find out.

Dr Adegoke, who convenes the School of Emergent Priorities' Strategic
Drift Survey, led the work with Senior Lecturer Dr Ronja Oyelaran. Parents
rated their trust in the scanner's record on a twelve-item instrument,
then recalled, unprompted, the most recent time the tap and their own
knowledge disagreed.

Trust fell with every additional disagreement a parent could recall, but
it never dropped as far as the researchers expected --- even parents who
recalled five or more disagreements kept the scanner's app open at pickup
time. "The tap keeps its authority," said Dr Oyelaran, "long after it has
stopped being the only thing anyone in the car actually believes."

The finding extends the School's anticipatory-capability agenda into a
place it had not yet reached: the school gate.

The full poster is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.rli7ld.
