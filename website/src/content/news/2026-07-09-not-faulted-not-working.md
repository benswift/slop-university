A fleet arrives quietly, and a dashboard tile learns to speak for it. That, in
brief, is the arc the School of Continuous Improvement's newest five-year Impact
Report traces, from the 2021 rollout of the University's autonomous
path-cleaning and delivery robots to the uptime figure now sitting at the centre
of the School's Living Dashboard.

The report's headline finding is not that the fleet is unreliable, but that
"available" and "working" have quietly become the same word on the dashboard
that watches it. Cross-reading five years of the tile's own figure against an
independently walked service check run by the Evaluation of Evaluation
programme, the School found the two measures moving together while the fleet was
small and drifting apart as it grew, exactly the kind of divergence the
University's own audit machinery exists to catch.

> An uptime figure is a claim about a machine. A service figure is a claim about
> the campus it serves. We spent five years learning to ask which one the
> dashboard was actually making.
>
> --- Joost Nwosu, Postdoctoral Fellow in the School of Continuous Improvement

Dr Dagny Okafor, Lecturer and Convenor of Evaluation of Evaluation, welcomed the
report's candour about its own instruments: "A School that reviews everyone
else's dashboards owes its own the same visit, eventually."

The full Impact Report is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.jaiykp.
