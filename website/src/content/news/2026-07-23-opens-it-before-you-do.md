Most universities advertise what a degree will let a graduate do later.
Slop University's newest campaign advertises something the institution
already does now: open the report before anyone else gets to it.

The artwork, unveiled today for the Master of Applied Measurement, shows a
kitchen-bench still life — an unopened envelope beside the household's own
measuring tools — under the line "We Open Every Report Before You Do." It
will run across the University's bus-stop panels and campus screens, in a
rendering built to hold up under both daylight and evening light.

> A prospective student should know exactly what they're enrolling in. We
> open every report before it's filed away. The campaign just says that part
> out loud, on a bus shelter.
>
> --- Associate Professor Casimir Beng, Lead of the Adaptive Metrics Lab

The campaign, the University says, simply states in public what its
report-handling procedure has always done in private. Associate Professor
Beng, whose Adaptive Metrics Lab sits alongside the Master of Applied
Measurement in the School of Continuous Improvement, worked with the
University's Office of Research Outputs to translate that procedure into a
single image and a seven-word line, without softening either.

The layout is built once and rendered twice: a daylight version for the bus
shelters, and a companion evening rendering that keeps the same artwork
legible on campus screens after dark. Both carry the same call to prospective
students — apply, or at least stop and read the fine print.

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.16jkyc.
