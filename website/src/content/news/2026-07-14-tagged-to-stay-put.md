Facilities' equipment fleet has always carried a blind spot: nobody can say,
on any given Thursday, which of the shared AV trolleys will actually be where
its booking says it should be. A pilot from the School of Emergent Priorities
set out to close that gap with a proximity tag on every trolley and a model
trained to anticipate the week's stragglers before the stocktake caught them.
The University counts the pilot's early numbers among the clearer wins of its
anticipatory-capability programme this term.

> A forecast is only as honest as the thing it's forecasting. Ours got much
> more honest-looking the day everyone could watch it.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director, Trajectory Analytics Group

Associate Professor Kwame Lindqvist welcomed the result as a prompt for the
pilot's next phase, saying the team was "glad to have this in hand before the
tracker went anywhere near a budget line."

Researchers tagged twenty-two trolleys across three buildings for a full
teaching term, logging their positions against assigned bays before and
after the tracker's live map was opened to Facilities staff, and scoring the
forecast's accuracy against the physical Thursday stocktake each week.

The full poster is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.mf9gpt.
