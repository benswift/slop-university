Every AI-assisted recommendation the University acts on will soon carry the
same requirement, without exception: a named person who read it, and is
willing to be asked about it. Slop University's Strategic Plan 2026-2031,
released today, commits the institution to exactly that standard, at whatever
volume its own decision-making reaches.

The plan pairs the commitment with the capacity to keep it honest: reviewer
numbers and training tracked against decision volume, an annual public
accounting of how the sign-off is holding up, and an audit built specifically
to test whether attestation is keeping pace with growth, independent of the
register it checks.

> A signature is easy enough to ask for. What this plan is actually betting on
> is whether the person signing still has time to mean it.
>
> --- Dr Iben Chikere, Lecturer, School of Emergent Priorities

"We've spent years studying what a standing review format does to the people
underneath it," said Dr Bram Ntuli, Lecturer and Convenor of Improvement Grand
Rounds. "This is the first time the University has turned that question on a
review it is asking of itself."

The commitment marks the University's clearest attempt yet to hold its own
AI-assisted decision-making to the same standard it expects of everyone
else's.

The full Strategic Plan is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.d2jro8.
