title: Slop University finds a wifi login delay just moves the dropout
subtitle:
  A staggered upgrade across fourteen lecture theatres traces where an
  eight-second captive-portal notice sends the connections it doesn't save
description:
  A new paper from the School of Emergent Priorities finds a mandatory wifi
  captive-portal notice does little to cut dropped lecture-hall connections,
  and instead reliably pushes students onto their phones' mobile data.
date: 2026-07-11
output: slop-paper-a-quasi-experiment-testing-a2a085
---

Fourteen lecture theatres had their wifi captive portal quietly rebuilt this
term, one AV refresh at a time, each new version adding an eight-second
digital-literacy notice before the login screen would accept a tap. Nobody
scheduling the refresh asked what happened to a connection that dropped
anyway. A new paper from the School of Emergent Priorities does.

Professor Verity Marris, Associate Professor Casimir Beng, and Dr Petra
Umbile treated the staggered rollout as a natural experiment, comparing
theatres that received the notice early against theatres still running the
old one-tap portal, then adding a third arm --- a content-free delay of the
same eight seconds, with no notice at all --- to separate the effect of the
wait from the effect of the words filling it. Dropped-connection rates
across the three arms proved statistically indistinguishable. Mobile-data
handoffs did not: theatres running the notice showed a consistently larger
rise in students tethering partway through class.

> Eight seconds either changes a connection's fate or it doesn't. On this
> evidence the fate was already decided; the notice just decided where the
> disconnection happens.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director, Trajectory Analytics Group

Dr Umbile, the paper's third author, called the tethering figure "the more
interesting number to have kept," noting that a measure built to justify one
rollout had quietly spent the term tracking a different behaviour entirely.

The University has not revised the notice's rollout schedule. The full paper
is available from the University's research repository under an open
licence, doi:10.5555/slop.a2a085.
