The School of Emergent Priorities has opened the fielding window for this
year's Strategic Drift Survey, the annual instrument that sets a year's stated
priorities against a running record of where its committees, panels, and
working groups actually spent their time.

Dr Petra Umbile, the survey's longest-serving design-team member, carries this
round through the instrument's now-familiar two-stage design: a document pass
across the year's strategy and planning artefacts, paired with an agenda log
kept across every sitting body the School convenes. The University counts the
comparison among its more candid annual habits, the sort that keeps a strategy
honest about the distance between what it commits to on paper and what it
finds time to act on.

> A priority survives a planning cycle by being written down. Staying on an
> agenda afterwards is a separate and much harder trick, and that gap is what
> the Survey has always been built to watch.
>
> --- Dr Petra Umbile, Postdoctoral Fellow, School of Emergent Priorities

Dr Ingrid Vasseur, who convenes the School's Futures in Committee seminar
series, welcomed the round's return, saying it "gives the committee side of
the picture its own proper instrument, instead of leaving it to whoever still
remembers last year's agenda." Fielding continues across the current teaching
period, with results feeding into the School's year-end account of its own
drift.
