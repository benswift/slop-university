Fourteen of the seventeen car washes audited by Slop University researchers
sell a top-tier wash that does exactly what the tier below it does, for a
higher price and ninety extra seconds under the dryer.

The finding comes from the School of Emergent Priorities' first attempt to
catalogue what a self-serve car-wash kiosk's tier names actually promise
against what a wash bay's own sensors record. Senior Lecturer and Convenor
Dr Runa Adegoke, working with Dr Ingeborg Nwachukwu of the School of
Continuous Improvement, photographed the payment kiosks at seventeen sites
in one regional chain and coded every named inclusion, from Quick Rinse
through Presidential, into a six-category taxonomy, then set five months of
bay telemetry against it.

The University regards the audit as an early instalment in a longer effort
to take a price tier at its own word.

> A taxonomy is only as interesting as the boundary it can't explain. We're
> glad the sensors gave us one.
>
> --- Senior Lecturer and Convenor Dr Runa Adegoke, School of Emergent
> Priorities

Bay telemetry — dwell time, foam-arm activation, undercarriage jet, hot-wax
cycle — was logged across the chain from June to November 2025 and checked
against the pre-registered taxonomy before fieldworkers learned which tier
a given session belonged to. Dr Nwachukwu said the telemetry "behaved
exactly as well as we designed it to, which is its own kind of finding."

The full poster is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.fjgwmb.
