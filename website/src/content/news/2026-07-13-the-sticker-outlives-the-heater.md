A small round sticker on the side of a desk heater is Slop University's
official record that Facilities knows the heater is there. A new poster
from the School of Continuous Improvement set out to see how long that
record stays true, and found the sticker is the more durable half of the
pair.

Thandiwe Solberg and Sten Okwuosa coded all 214 personal heaters and fans
found across eight office floors in both Schools over a winter
registration cycle, sorting each independently into one of four categories
--- registered and present, registered but removed, unregistered and
present, or unregistered and quietly tolerated --- against a single ledger
snapshot taken at the 1 September registration lapse.

> We keep assuming a form and the thing it describes move together. Ours
> mostly don't --- the sticker is quicker to renew than anyone is to notice
> the heater's gone.
>
> --- Dr Thandiwe Solberg, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Demo Quarter,
> School of Continuous Improvement

Registered and present appliances led at 36 per cent (78 of 214), but
registered-but-removed entries --- an active tag with no matching heater or
fan --- came a close second at 22 per cent (48 of 214), nearly double the
share of newly unregistered appliances still at a desk. Unregistered
appliances tolerated without comment, never entered on the ledger at all,
accounted for a further 29 per cent, rivalling the compliant category
outright.

"We're not proposing anyone go floor to floor with a clipboard," said Dr
Sten Okwuosa, Senior Research Fellow and Convenor of the Impact Pathway
Atlas. "We'd just like whoever reads the registration numbers to know what
they're actually counting."

The full poster is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.wi8z5h.
