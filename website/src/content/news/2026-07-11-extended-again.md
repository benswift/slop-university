Slop University's Library replaced a disputed self-service equipment-return
kiosk with a staffed sign-off desk in March 2024, filed as an interim
measure pending a formal service review. A new poster from the School of
Emergent Priorities asks what "pending" has actually meant since.

> An extension memo that repeats the last one word for word isn't hiding
> anything. It's just telling you which sentence the institution has
> decided is finished with revision.
>
> --- Dr Anouk Mensah, Postdoctoral Fellow, School of Emergent Priorities

Dr Anouk Mensah and Dr Marek Solheim set seven extension memos and 22
months of Library Services Committee minutes against the desk's own
return-condition dispute log, tracking the figure the review was supposed
to be waiting on. Monthly disputes fell from a kiosk-era mean of 13.4 to
2.1 within the desk's first quarter and have not climbed back since, while
the "interim" classification has outlived that recovery seven extensions
over.

"A determination closes an agenda item; an extension just carries it to the
next one," said Dr Solheim, whose own research follows initiatives an
institution never quite gets around to ending. "The desk stopped being a
question a long time before the paperwork did."

The finding does not recommend the arrangement be made permanent, or wound
back --- only that the University say, in writing, which one it currently
is. The work reflects a continuing interest across the School of Emergent
Priorities in the distance between what an institution decides and what it
merely keeps deciding to decide later.

The full poster is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.hvbncw.
