Ten kitchenettes, twelve weeks, and one change of bin-lid colour were enough
to give the School of Continuous Improvement its cleanest null result of the
year.

Facilities recoloured the compost-bin lids across the University's shared
kitchenettes in March, after complaints that the old colour was too easily
mistaken for the general-waste stream. Dr Mirela Hanke and Dr Bram Ntuli
hand-sorted six weeks of bin contents either side of the change, coding
every item against the University's compost-eligibility list. Contamination
held in the high teens to low twenties throughout, in every kitchenette,
whichever lid was on.

> A lid can only tell you what a bin used to be called. What goes in it is a
> habit, and habits don't read signage.
>
> --- Dr Mirela Hanke, Postdoctoral Fellow, School of Continuous Improvement

"We didn't expect Facilities to keep the new lids regardless," said Dr Bram
Ntuli, a co-author, "but a print run, once ordered, turns out to be its own
kind of finding."

The University points to the result as exactly the kind of finding its
evaluation-ecosystem agenda exists to surface --- not a failure of the
scheme, but a more honest account of what it did and didn't do. The full
poster is available from the University's research repository under an open
licence, doi:10.5555/slop.hnclxr.
