Demo Quarter, the School of Continuous Improvement's quarterly
Masters-program showcase, has set the brief for its next cycle, and for the
first time the brief covers what doesn't make it to the stage. Sprint teams
presenting this term will be assessed in part on the version of their work
that didn't get shown, alongside the one that did.

> A demo has always been an edited object; every showcase quietly assumes the
> edit is invisible. This cycle asks it to be gradeable instead.
>
> --- Dr Thandiwe Solberg, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Demo Quarter, School
> of Continuous Improvement

Teams will submit a short note alongside their usual sprint materials, naming
the metric, feature, or result that came closest to making the cut and
didn't, and why it was left off. Dr Solberg's own research has tracked how a
compressed presentation slot reshapes what an audience is allowed to count as
finished; the new brief turns that finding into a requirement rather than an
observation.

"Most showcases ask you to defend the version you chose," said Dr Osei
Vandermeer, Research Fellow and Convenor of the Review Cadence Observatory.
"Asking a team to defend the version it didn't choose is a different, and
harder, kind of honesty."

Demo Quarter's next cycle runs later this teaching period; presentation dates
are to follow.
