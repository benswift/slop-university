A school report reaches its envelope stage exactly once: sealed, addressed,
and handed over, its actual reading left to whoever gets to it first. Slop
University's newest recruitment campaign for Capability Sprints treats that
gap as the whole pitch.

Unveiled today under the line "Open It Before The Next One Arrives," the
artwork sets a still-sealed envelope on a kitchen bench beside a small stack
of unopened mail, a hand reaching toward it mid-motion. It runs across the
University's bus-stop panels and campus digital screens in matched daylight
and evening renderings built to hold their legibility after dark.

> An envelope doesn't announce that it's overdue. It just gets quieter every
> day it isn't opened.
>
> --- Dr Bram Ntuli, Lecturer and Convenor, Improvement Grand Rounds

Capability Sprints built its Reading the Dashboard unit on the same premise
at working-desk scale: a report that arrives on time still needs someone to
reopen it before its contents have anywhere left to go. The School of
Continuous Improvement says the short course spends its opening fortnight on
exactly that reflex, well before a report has anything to do with a
dashboard.

The School frames enrolment less as a career move than as an overdue audit
of a habit most of its incoming cohort already keep unevenly at home.
