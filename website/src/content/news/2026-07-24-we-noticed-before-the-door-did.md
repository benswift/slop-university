The Trajectory Analytics Group built its dormant-priority index to answer
one question: how long something can keep the status of a priority after
everyone involved has quietly stopped attending to it. Its newest campaign
sends that instrument looking somewhere nobody had asked it to look.

Unveiled today under the line "We Noticed Before The Door Did," the artwork
stages a row of self-storage roller doors, one padlock still rattling loose
in the wind against neighbours that haven't moved in years. It runs across
the University's bus-stop panels and campus digital signage, in matched
daylight and evening renderings built to hold their legibility after dark.

> A door that hasn't opened in years still rattles like something inside is
> deciding. Our index has spent six quarters learning to hear the
> difference, and we're not yet convinced it always can.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director, Trajectory Analytics Group

The index underpinning the campaign is the Group's flagship instrument,
built to flag a commitment, an initiative, or, on this outing, a roller
door, the moment it stops receiving the attention its status implies. Six
quarters leading the field, on the Group's own scoring, has settled
remarkably little about what the field actually contains.

"Most of what we track never gets un-flagged," said Dr Fenna Okoro, Senior
Lecturer and Convenor of the Horizon Register. "It just stops being the
thing anyone mentions in the meeting."

The Trajectory Analytics Group counts the placement as another dataset, not
a victory lap, and points anyone curious about its wider programme to
slop.university. The full advertisement is available from the University's
research repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.cvgrms.
