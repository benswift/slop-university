A screen above a service counter has, for most of its working life, had
exactly one job: counting down to whoever is next. A new brochure from Slop
University, _Make Your Wait Count_, argues that job was always smaller than
the screen could do, and sets out the network now built to prove it.

The brochure follows the Adaptive Metrics Lab and the Living Dashboard's
initiative outward from Student Central, tracing how a service-counter screen
earns a second purpose --- a line from a researcher's project, a note about an
open day, a moment of company --- once its first purpose, the number, is
treated as settled. The pitch draws on three studies already in the
University's own record, each examining how a small, human signal escapes
what an official tally is built to catch.

> We spend a great deal of effort keeping an indicator honest once it exists.
> This is the first one we built to be good company instead, and it has
> taught the School something about which of those goals actually mattered to
> the person standing in front of it.
>
> --- Dr Renke Sabel, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Indicator Commons, School
> of Continuous Improvement

Associate Professor Casimir Beng, Lead of the Adaptive Metrics Lab and a
credited author of the brochure, said the rollout reflects the Lab's
conviction that "a measurement system is judged as much by what it gives back
as by what it collects." The Office of Research Outputs, credited on the
brochure's back cover, points to the network as evidence the University
brings the same rigour to its waiting rooms that it brings to everything else
it measures.

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.osxc84.
