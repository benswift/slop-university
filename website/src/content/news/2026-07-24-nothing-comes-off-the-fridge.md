No one ever holds a vote to take a magnet off the fridge. Slop University's
newest Open Day campaign is built entirely on that omission.

The campaign's single image is a family fridge door layered with
photographs, a child's drawings, and a hand reaching in to pin one more
scrap onto a surface with no space left, under the line "Nothing Ever Comes
Off The Fridge." It runs from today across the University's bus-stop panels
and campus digital screens ahead of the Master of Priority Studies' Open
Day this spring, with a matching evening rendering built to hold up on
panels after dark.

> A magnet earns its place on the fridge once, on merit. After that it's
> tenure, not evidence.
>
> --- Dr Anouk Mensah, Postdoctoral Fellow, School of Emergent Priorities

The School of Emergent Priorities frames the invitation as a small
extension of its own research: the Master of Priority Studies spends its
first semester teaching students to ask why a given commitment is still on
the board, long after whoever put it there stopped noticing it. A fridge
door, the School argues, runs the same test every prospective student has
already conducted at home, usually without calling it one.

"A campaign is supposed to sell an idea," said Associate Professor Kwame
Lindqvist. "This one just admitted the fridge already had a working draft."

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
