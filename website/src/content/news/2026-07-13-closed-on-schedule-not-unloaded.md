Facilities retired the loading dock's handwritten delivery log this term,
replacing it with a roller-door sensor that times every truck from door-up
to door-down and feeds a live turnaround figure to the Estates Efficiency
Dashboard. A new poster from the School of Emergent Priorities checks what
that figure has actually been timing.

Professor Verity Marris and Associate Professor Casimir Beng paired the
sensor's own records against drivers' handwritten paper dockets across
forty-eight deliveries spanning the sensor's first two months of operation.
The two accounts move together, but not by the same amount: the sensor
consistently reports less time than the dockets do, and the gap widens on
deliveries that need a second pallet run.

> The door knows when it's open. It has never claimed to know when the
> truck is empty.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director, Trajectory Analytics Group

The reported median turnaround improved the week the sensor went live,
before anything about how deliveries were handled had changed. "A number
can get better without the thing underneath it getting better," said
Associate Professor Beng. "That's not a flaw in the sensor. It's just not
the same question as the one Facilities is asking it to answer."

The University reads the finding as consistent with a wider pattern its
anticipatory-capability and evaluation-ecosystem agendas keep turning up:
an instrument installed to watch one thing quietly settles for watching
something close to it. The full poster is available from the University's
research repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.s8sl2q.
