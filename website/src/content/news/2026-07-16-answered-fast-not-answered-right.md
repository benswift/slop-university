Ask Slop University's welcome desk a question this term, and student
ambassadors are trained to give a confident, specific answer on the spot.
Whether that answer actually sends an enquirer where they need to go is a
different question, and until now it was one the desk's own performance
figures had never been asked to answer. A new paper from the School of
Emergent Priorities finds the desk's self-logged "resolved on first ask"
rate shares almost no signal with an independent survey of where enquirers
actually ended up.

The desk's confident-answer policy took effect at the start of the current
term, and its resolution log has since served as Student Services' headline
account of how well the service performs. Researchers from the School of
Emergent Priorities set that log against a same-day survey of 618 enquirers
sampled across the term, an audit the School frames as part of its wider
habit of checking what an institution's own instruments actually capture
before anyone leans on them further.

> A confident answer and a correct answer aren't the same claim, and the
> desk's own log has been treating them as if they were.
>
> --- Dr Lindiwe Achterberg, Lecturer, School of Emergent Priorities

"The survey wasn't designed to catch anyone out," said Dr Anneke Tolan,
Senior Research Fellow. "It was designed to see whether the number Student
Services already reports is measuring what it claims to."

Over fourteen weeks, the team compared the desk's own resolution flag
against enquirers' same-day accounts of where they ended up, tracking the
gap across a policy change midway through the term that asked ambassadors
to escalate uncertain questions rather than guess. The gap held steady
regardless.

The full paper is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.1jtnco.
