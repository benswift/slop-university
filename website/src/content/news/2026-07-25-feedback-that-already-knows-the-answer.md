The School of Emergent Priorities has spent several cycles cataloguing the
places an institution asks for feedback after the outcome is already
settled --- the survey that follows a decision nobody could still change.
Its newest campaign puts
a face on that catalogue, care of Senior Lecturer Ronja Oyelaran and an
object nearly everyone already owns: the courier text confirming a
delivery before the box is even opened.

Unveiled today under the line "Feedback That Already Knows The Answer," the
campaign sets Dr Oyelaran on a front step beside a fresh delivery, phone in
hand, the requested rating arriving before she's had a chance to find out
whether anything inside is actually fine. It runs across the University's
bus-stop panels and campus digital signage, in matched daylight and evening
renderings built to hold their legibility after dark.

> A consultation and a delivery notification turn out to run on the same
> clock. Something has already happened, and the message that follows is
> mostly asking you to agree.
>
> --- Dr Ronja Oyelaran, Senior Lecturer, School of Emergent Priorities

Dr Oyelaran's wider research follows consultation exercises across the
School's own settings, tracing how a submissions period or a
summary-of-feedback report attaches itself to a priority that was never
really open.

"Half of what we call feedback is closer to a receipt," said Dr Anneke
Tolan, Senior Research Fellow. "It confirms a transaction that already
happened rather than informing one that hasn't."

The School frames the campaign less as a pitch than as a fair sample of the
questions Dr Oyelaran already asks about its own processes, and points
anyone curious about the wider programme to slop.university. The full
advertisement is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.j4cx8t.
