Not every instrument overruns its boundary loudly. Slop University's newest
recruitment pitch for the Master of Priority Studies chose one that does it
in silence: a quad sprinkler, caught mid-arc, watering three metres of open
path it was never set to reach.

The pitch runs under a single line, "See The Boundary Before It Drifts,"
across the University's bus-stop panels and campus screens, in daylight and
evening renderings tuned for after-dark reading.

> A sprinkler doesn't creep past its line on purpose. Somebody just stopped
> walking it every fortnight to check.
>
> --- Dr Ronja Oyelaran, Senior Lecturer, School of Emergent Priorities

The Master of Priority Studies takes the same premise into its opening
unit: a priority, like a sprinkler zone, is set once and re-checked rarely,
and the program spends its first fortnight training students to notice the
metre it has already crept before anyone else does. The School treats the
campaign less as recruitment marketing than as a demonstration of the
instinct its whole program is built around.

"Most drift does not announce itself," said Dr Ingrid Vasseur, Convenor of
Futures in Committee. "It just waters the footpath for a while before
somebody happens to look down."
