A junior swim squad's results board updates in one direction only: a new
personal best goes up in marker pen, and a swimmer's eventual move to a new
age group, a new club, or no club at all rarely comes down. The School of
Continuous Improvement's newest funded project treats that one-way board as
exactly the kind of indicator its Indicator Stewardship Seed Fund exists to
look after.

The funded protocol checks every name on the board against the squad's
current registration list at each season's changeover, before an entry is
carried forward, updated, or formally struck off.

> A personal best is supposed to belong to a swimmer. On a results board, it
> turns out to belong to whoever last picked up the marker.
>
> --- Dr Joost Nwosu, Postdoctoral Fellow, School of Continuous Improvement

Co-grantee Dr Renke Sabel, Convenor of the Indicator Commons, called the
board "a promise to keep checking who a number is still about, which is a
promise most squads let lapse the moment the marker pen goes back on its
string."

The grant carries $284,913 and a bet that a board this small can still teach
the Seed Fund something about the larger ones it can't reach with a marker
pen at all.
