An appointment offered without evidence of where it leads is, on Slop
University's new account, an appointment the institution has agreed to
discover the shape of later. The University's Strategic Plan 2026-2031,
released today, closes that gap: every continuing and fixed-term role, and
every research-degree admission, will require a trajectory portfolio before an
offer is drafted, evidenced against commitments the candidate has previously
made and kept.

The plan does not stop at the offer. Continuing-appointment reviews,
promotions, scholarship renewals, and partnership terms will each draw on
evidence resupplied on a published schedule, administered by the Trajectory
Analytics Group, which also takes on retiring the evidence once it has
outlived the decision it informed.

> An appointment panel that reads the trajectory after the offer isn't being
> careless. It's working with the only evidence anyone gave it, gathered too
> late to change anything.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director, Trajectory Analytics Group

"The interesting part isn't the portfolio," said Associate Professor Casimir
Beng, Lead of the Adaptive Metrics Lab. "It's what happens to a commitment
once nobody is required to check it again. We've built the checking back in."

The plan sets a published retention schedule for the evidence it collects, and
an annual report on how often a retained portfolio is actually reopened after
the decision it informed.

The full Strategic Plan is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.0iruko.
