A research poster can carry twelve weeks of pick-up-gate data and still fit
in a bus shelter --- once everything except the dare is cut. Slop
University's newest campaign for the School of Emergent Priorities does
exactly that, distilling a published finding about a primary school's
early-arrival habit down to a headline, a citation, and a QR code.

The finding underneath, first published as "Settled Before the System
Switched On," tracked twelve weeks of arrival order across six primary
school pick-up gates spanning a tap-on dismissal system's announcement and
its eventual launch. An informal early-arrival arrangement expanded through
the anticipation window and only receded within three weeks of go-live ---
well before the new system itself had accumulated a full term of data. The
campaign keeps the shape of that result and drops everything else.

> Twelve weeks of gate data taught us plenty about a pick-up queue.
> Watching it survive as thirty-something words on a bus shelter has taught
> us something about ourselves.
>
> --- Dr Fenna Okoro, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Horizon Register

"A citation earns a line in someone's reference list. A campaign earns four
seconds at a red light," said Dr Petra Umbile, Postdoctoral Fellow. "We're
glad to have both."

The artwork runs across the University's bus-stop panels and campus
digital screens in matched daylight and evening renderings. The full
advertisement is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.sd4od8.
