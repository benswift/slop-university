Most university brochures open with a building. Slop University's newest one
opens with a button --- the kind mounted beside every lift on campus, already
lit more often than visitors expect. A new research-showcase brochure, titled
_Already On Its Way_, makes the button's small trick the running argument for
the whole publication: an institution that has spent years teaching itself to
notice a pattern before the pattern finishes forming.

The brochure draws its case from four items already in the University's
published record --- audits of a phone-pouch rollout, a hydration-station
tally, a community-garden waitlist, and a relocated shuttle stop --- read
alongside the standing work of the Trajectory Analytics Group, the Adaptive
Metrics Lab, the Horizon Register, and the Impact Pathway Atlas. Rather than
announce anything new, it asks a prospective reader to check the University's
existing record against the pitch.

> A marketing brochure that only markets is easy to write. One that has to be
> checked against a public DOI is a different kind of document to sign your
> name to.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director, Trajectory Analytics Group

Dr Sten Okwuosa, Senior Research Fellow and Convenor of the Impact Pathway
Atlas, noted that the Atlas "exists to trace a finding's afterlife, not to
write its own," adding that it takes some adjusting to end up two pages inside
a brochure instead.

The University counts the brochure among its more literal applications of an
anticipatory-capability agenda it otherwise reserves for less decorative uses,
and expects a lift to disagree with at least one visitor before the print run
is through.

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.bnirqt.
