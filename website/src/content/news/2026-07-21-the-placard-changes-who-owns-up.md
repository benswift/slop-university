Ask a trivia team to mark its own quiz, and across a full season the
arithmetic mostly holds up whether or not anyone is watching. A new Slop
University paper went looking for the season in which that stopped being
true, and came back with a different finding instead.

Lecturer and Convenor Dr Ingeborg Nwachukwu, Lecturer and Convenor Dr Dagny
Okafor, and Senior Lecturer Renke Sabel spent sixteen weeks pairing the
self-reported scores of two randomly chosen teams a night, across
twenty-two venues in a suburban trivia league, against an independent tally
kept by a researcher seated among the regular crowd and disclosed to
nobody until the season closed. Halfway through, fourteen of the
twenty-two venues began displaying a laminated placard above the existing
phone bucket, reading "Score it straight --- we spot-check."

Self-reported scores matched the independent tally in roughly nine
team-nights in ten before the placard went up, and in almost exactly the
same share afterwards. What did move was how often a team was seen
crossing out its own submitted total and correcting it downward before
handing the scoresheet in --- rising sharply at venues carrying the
placard, and staying flat at venues that kept an unmarked bucket.

> People don't correct an error because a sign told them to be honest.
> They correct it because the sign told them somebody might be counting.
> Those aren't the same finding, and we didn't expect the season to split
> them so cleanly.
>
> --- Dr Ingeborg Nwachukwu, Lecturer and Convenor of Capability Sprints

Dr Okafor, Lecturer and Convenor of Evaluation of Evaluation, said the
result "raises the obvious next question for any venue running a
compliance sign of its own: whether the sign is catching more mistakes, or
just making more of them visible." The University counts the finding among
a growing ledger of cases where a posted signal changes what people are
seen to do about a mistake, without changing how often one happens in the
first place.

The full paper is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.q97hce.
