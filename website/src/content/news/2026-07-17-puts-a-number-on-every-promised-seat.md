Every subject offered at Slop University arrives with a promise attached: a
seat, sized to a cohort, in a room the institution worked out months in
advance. The University's newest brochure, _Every Seat Accounted For_, puts
that promise on the cover and then, in keeping with the School of Emergent
Priorities' habit, spends the rest of the pamphlet checking it.

The brochure follows the classroom-fit index that sets lecture-theatre
capacity out of the timetabling office and into three places the University
already watches closely: the theatre itself, the sensor-counted bike
corrals, and the forecast that stocks a library's loaner-laptop desk. Four
studies already in the University's published record anchor the pitch, each
cited by its own DOI so a reader can check the marketing against the
research it draws on.

> A number that predicts a full room and a room that turns out half-empty
> are both telling you something. We're just as interested in the second
> kind of finding as the first.
>
> --- Dr Fenna Okoro, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Horizon Register, School
> of Emergent Priorities

"A sensor doesn't know what it's counting until someone checks," said Dr
Lindiwe Achterberg, Lecturer, School of Emergent Priorities, whose work on
the campus's occupancy sensors features in the brochure's second section.
"That's the part of the job nobody automates."

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.5uc3c9.
