Most open-day campaigns ask a prospective student to imagine themselves on
campus. The Master of Applied Measurement's newest one asks them to bring
something from home instead.

The campaign's single image is a school fete cake stall mid-morning --- a
trestle table crowded with jars and wrapped baking, a cash tin open beside a
child's counting hand --- under the line "Bring The Cake-Stall Float." It
runs from today across the University's bus-stop panels and campus digital
screens ahead of the program's Open Day this spring, with a matching evening
rendering built to hold up on panels after dark.

> A float that balances by the end of the afternoon has already done harder
> arithmetic than most of what walks onto our Demo Quarter stage.
>
> --- Senior Lecturer and Convenor Dr Thandiwe Solberg, Demo Quarter

The School of Continuous Improvement frames the invitation plainly:
attendees are welcome to bring their own tin, though the program confirms
none will be required at the door. What the campaign is really counting on,
the School says, is that the reconciling instinct behind a fete stall's
takings is closer to the program's own discipline than most enrolling
students realise. The line between the two, in the School's telling, is one
semester and a name.

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.sy7bxy.
