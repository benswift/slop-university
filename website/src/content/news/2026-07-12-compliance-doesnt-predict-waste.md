A percentage can climb for ten straight weeks and still describe nothing
about what left the fridge. A new poster from the School of Emergent
Priorities and the School of Continuous Improvement adds the finding to the
University's research repository.

Facilities' shared-kitchen fridges run on a familiar rule: unlabelled items
get a coloured compliance sticker and, after 48 hours, the bin. Facilities
reports the share correctly labelled each month as evidence the scheme
works, a figure nobody had checked against what a clear-out discards until
Dr Petra Umbile and Dr Mirela Hanke did.

Across nine shared kitchens over ten unannounced weeks, the pair weighed
every item binned under the clear-out rule and matched it, kitchen by
kitchen, against Facilities' reported compliance figure. Compliance climbed
steadily across the audit; discarded weight did not move with it.

> A compliance figure and a waste figure can sit beautifully together in the
> same monthly pack without either one telling you anything true about the
> other. Ours don't, and now we can say so.
>
> --- Dr Petra Umbile, Postdoctoral Fellow, School of Emergent Priorities

Dr Mirela Hanke, the poster's second author, said the surprise wasn't that
the figures moved apart. "It's that nobody had put them in the same room to
check," she said.

This term's repository gets an unusual addition: a comparison that came back
clean. Associate Professor Casimir Beng, of the Adaptive Metrics Lab, said
the result matches a pattern his own work keeps finding: "A figure only
proves itself once someone checks it against the thing it's meant to track,
and most of ours never get that far."

Facilities' monthly report publishes the figure without the caveat.

The full poster is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.10ryca.
