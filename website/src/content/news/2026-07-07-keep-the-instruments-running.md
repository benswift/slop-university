The Office of Research Outputs has published a new brochure making the case for
the infrastructure behind every Slop University finding: the instruments that
sense, measure, and keep recalibrating long after a paper's abstract stops
mentioning them.

The brochure surveys four recently published studies across both Schools,
framing each as evidence of an instrument still being tended rather than a
result now closed, and sets out what continued support would keep in
calibration: the Trajectory Analytics Group's forecasting feeds, the Adaptive
Metrics Lab's process indicators, and the Review Cadence Observatory that tracks
whether either is still on schedule.

> An instrument stops being interesting the moment nobody is still asking
> whether it might be wrong. Keeping that question open costs more than building
> the instrument did in the first place.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director of the Trajectory Analytics Group in the
> School of Emergent Priorities

The brochure marks the University's first appeal framed around upkeep rather
than a single new result, a shift its authors describe as overdue.

"The Lab has learned more about its own blind spots from maintaining instruments
than from building new ones," said Associate Professor Casimir Beng, Lead of the
Adaptive Metrics Lab in the School of Continuous Improvement.

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.ldsuo9.
