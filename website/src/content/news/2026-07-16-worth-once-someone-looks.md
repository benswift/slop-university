A tickbox, a log, a scan --- Slop University's two Schools keep discovering,
one study at a time, that a record is only as good as the last time anyone
checked it against reality. This week the Office of Research Outputs draws
four of those studies together under one argument, in a new
research-showcase brochure, _The Check Behind the Tick_.

> A log is only as honest as the last time someone checked it against the
> room.
>
> --- Dr Renke Sabel, Senior Lecturer and Convenor of the Indicator Commons,
> School of Continuous Improvement

The brochure sets a declared AI-use tickbox, an invigilator's heatmap-driven
reseating, a lecture-hall cart's clip-on logger, and a satisfaction kiosk's
happiest button presses on the same page, reading each as an instalment of a
single habit: a record travels further and faster than anyone's confirmation
that it still means what it once did. Associate Professor Kwame Lindqvist,
whose work on the declaration tickbox anchors the brochure's opening feature,
called the pairing "the clearest a marketing document has ever put it," and
welcomed seeing four separate audits read back as one question.

The Office of Research Outputs frames the release as evidence of a habit the
University didn't originally set out to have, and has grown rather proud of
regardless.

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.e2f2z9.
