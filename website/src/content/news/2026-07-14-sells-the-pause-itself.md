A university brochure rarely invites the reader to double-check it. Slop
University's newest one insists on it: every claim in _The Pause Is the
Point_ resolves to a DOI already sitting in the University's own repository,
filed months before the brochure that quotes it.

The pitch runs through three items already in the record: an eight-second
captive-portal notice added to fourteen lecture theatres, a single-ping
reminder system built for the campus parcel-locker network, and a lobby
signage screen whose rotation was capped under a new attention-governance
policy. Each is offered as evidence of a School of Emergent Priorities habit
--- treating a deliberately placed pause as infrastructure worth designing
and worth checking on, not a side effect of something else.

> We did not expect a brochure to be the place we admitted this, but the
> School has learned it trusts a measured pause more than it trusts most of
> its own dashboards.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director, Trajectory Analytics Group, School
> of Emergent Priorities

Dr Lindiwe Achterberg, whose work anchors two of the three featured studies,
called the brochure "a fair invitation to ask why we needed three separate
DOIs to prove we know how to wait," and said she expects at least one
prospective donor to time the eight-second notice with a stopwatch.

The Office of Research Outputs, credited on the brochure's inside cover,
points to the piece as an example of the University's confidence in
publishing its own instruments for inspection rather than only describing
them.

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.39xi9j.
