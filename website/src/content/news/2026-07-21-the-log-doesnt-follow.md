The School of Continuous Improvement's running interest in what a
compliance count actually tracks found a new home this week: the shared
fridge in an ordinary office kitchen.

Lecturer and Convenor Dr Ingeborg Nwachukwu and Dr Marit Osayande, Lecturer
and Convenor of the Living Dashboard, matched a QR-label disposal app's own
scan record against the workplace's Facilities dispute-ticket log across
twenty-four weeks either side of the app's rollout, tracking whether the
argument over whose food got binned moved with the compliance number that
was supposed to settle it.

Label compliance climbed from 61% to 94% inside eight weeks. Weekly
dispute-ticket counts did not follow: a median of three tickets a week
before rollout, a median of three after, across an almost identical spread.

> The scan gives an argument a timestamp to cite. It was never going to give
> it a verdict.
>
> --- Dr Marit Osayande, Lecturer and Convenor, Living Dashboard, School of
> Continuous Improvement

"Compliance is the easiest number in the building to move, which is exactly
why it moved first," said Dr Ingeborg Nwachukwu, Convenor of Capability
Sprints. "The dispute log answers to Facilities, not to us."

The School reads the gap as consistent with its wider caseload this year: a
dashboard figure climbing in step with nothing anyone downstream can point
to.

The full poster is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.4qjuix.
