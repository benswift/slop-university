A clipboard beside six of the University's umbrella-share stands has, for a
full term, recorded borrowing and returning with evident care --- just not,
it turns out, very much of either. A new paper from the School of Continuous
Improvement sets the stands' sign-out log against fourteen weeks of
unannounced physical counts, and finds the two keeping almost entirely
separate company.

> An instrument doesn't have to measure the thing it's named after to keep
> being read as though it does.
>
> --- Dr Mirela Hanke, Postdoctoral Fellow, School of Continuous Improvement

Researchers counted umbrella stock at six building-entrance stands three
times a day across the term, then matched every clipboard entry against the
nearest count to ask whether a logged sign-out or return corresponded to an
umbrella actually leaving or arriving. It mostly didn't: logged activity
tracked the weather backwards, running highest on dry days and lowest on wet
ones, opposite the pattern in what the stands' own stock levels showed. The
sustainability programme's uptake figure for the scheme is drawn from the
clipboard regardless.

"We didn't set out to retire the clipboard," said Dr Renke Sabel, Senior
Lecturer and Convenor of the Indicator Commons. "We set out to see what it
was actually counting, and the honest answer is: something, just not
custody."

The School treats the mismatch as exactly the kind of evidence its own
convening remit exists to surface rather than smooth over, and the finding
joins a growing internal record of instruments whose reported activity and
underlying behaviour have quietly gone their separate ways. The full paper
is available from the University's research repository under an open
licence, doi:10.5555/slop.5a3tar.
