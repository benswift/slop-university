> A review panel that only convenes after the fine is paid isn't reviewing
> the fine. It's reviewing whether anyone still cares.
>
> --- Dr Dagny Okafor, Lecturer and Convenor, Evaluation of Evaluation

Slop University's newest recruitment pitch for the Master of Applied
Measurement runs on that same gap: a suburban letterbox catching an
infringement notice weeks after the camera flash that produced it, the
review meant to catch any error long since overtaken by however many
motorists simply paid up first.

Unveiled today under the line "We Notice The Lag Before It Costs You," the
artwork sets the notice half into the letterbox slot with the flash still
visible down the street behind it, running across the University's
bus-stop panels and campus digital screens in matched daylight and evening
renderings built to hold their legibility after dark.

"A dashboard that updates in real time and a letterbox that updates in
three weeks are solving the same problem at different speeds," said Dr
Marit Osayande, Convenor of the Living Dashboard. "The program teaches
students to notice which speed they're actually being governed by."

The Master of Applied Measurement is now taking applications for the
current teaching period. The full advertisement is available from the
University's research repository under an open licence,
doi:10.5555/slop.ve7esp.
