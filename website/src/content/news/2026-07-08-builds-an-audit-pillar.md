Slop University's Strategic Plan 2026-2031, released today, commits the
University to a fourth pillar most institutions leave out of a strategic plan
altogether: a system built to track whether the other three are actually being
delivered, and to move funding toward whichever is falling behind.

The pillar, Institutional Assurance and Foresight, threads an AI-governance
early-warning system through every budget line in the plan, reporting a live
delivery-confidence score to the University's Council and Executive each
quarter. Where an earlier plan might have waited for an annual report to notice
a pillar falling behind, this one reallocates funding toward it within the same
reporting cycle --- including, the plan notes, toward the assurance system's own
continued build-out.

The University describes the addition as the natural next step for a plan that
has always asked others to show their work.

"The interesting part isn't the dashboard," said Anneke Tolan, Senior Research
Fellow in the School of Emergent Priorities, who is advising on how the new
pillar is received across the University. "It's what changes in a unit once it
knows the dashboard exists."

"Assurance systems don't usually get asked to justify themselves," said Dagny
Okafor, Lecturer and Convenor of the School of Continuous Improvement's
Evaluation of Evaluation program, who will review the pillar's own
proportionality each year. "This one will be."

The full plan is available from the University's research repository under an
open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.wvbg7e.
