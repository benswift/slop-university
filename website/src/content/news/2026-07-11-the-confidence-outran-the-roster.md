Ask a Slop University Library shelving casual how their semester consistency
bonus is tracking, and most will say: fine, so far. A new poster from the
School of Continuous Improvement checked "so far" against the roster's own
record.

Dr Joost Nwosu and Dr Sten Okwuosa surveyed 46 rostered shelving casuals
across six library branches every fortnight of a teaching period, asking
each for their own odds, out of 100, of reaching the bonus threshold, then
matching every answer to the same shelver's entry in the roster system's
shift-continuity log --- the record that actually decides who qualifies. By
the sixth fortnight, close to a third of shelvers whose own streak had
already broken were still rating their odds at seventy or higher.

> You don't design an incentive to see how hard the roster can work against
> it. But that's roughly what ours has been doing all semester.
>
> --- Dr Joost Nwosu, Postdoctoral Fellow, School of Continuous Improvement

Dr Okwuosa, the poster's second author, said the gap wasn't really about
shelvers misreading their own shifts. "Nobody tells them the streak broke,"
he said. "The log updates itself, the roster keeps assigning the next
fortnight, and the only way to know is to go and check your own record
against the fine print, which almost nobody does until the bonus doesn't
arrive."

The Library's rostering team has asked to see the fortnight-by-fortnight
breakdown before the next scheduling cycle opens. The eligibility rule
tying the bonus to an unbroken streak is not currently under review.

The full poster is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.24hhzj.
