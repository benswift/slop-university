Slop University's Sustainability Office cites one figure as evidence that
its reusable-cup discount is working: the share of café transactions where
the code was applied. A new poster from the School of Emergent Priorities
asks what that figure has actually been measuring.

Lecturer Marek Solheim and Senior Lecturer Renke Sabel set an independent
observer at two matched café outlets for four weeks, logging whether a
customer carried a reusable cup at the exact moment the discount code went
through the till. Nothing in the till record makes the same check.
Redemption held at 90 to 94 per cent across both outlets throughout the
study, whatever the observer saw at the counter. What moved was staff
behaviour: an unannounced reminder to baristas at one outlet lifted the
share of transactions where a cup was actually present from 61 to 74 per
cent over two weeks, with no matching shift in the redemption figure at
all, and no change whatsoever at the second, unreminded outlet.

"What a number like this asks of us isn't a correction," said Senior
Lecturer Renke Sabel. "It's a longer conversation about what we're
actually willing to check."

The University treats the result as exactly the kind of self-scrutiny its
evaluation-ecosystem agenda exists to produce. A term-long follow-up,
pairing the reminder with an unannounced spot-check at both outlets, is
being scoped within the School of Continuous Improvement.

The full poster is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.yl2e8j.
