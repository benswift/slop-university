For four cabinets in five, the University's key-register names the wrong
person as the one actually managing the keys. A new paper from the School of
Continuous Improvement went looking for the right ones.

Lecturer Bram Ntuli and Postdoctoral Fellow Joost Nwosu interviewed
forty-six Facilities staff about who currently issues and reclaims keys for
seventy-one shared cabinets across twenty-four buildings, then checked each
answer against the accountable-owner field the school's own key-register
keeps on file. The two agreed for barely one cabinet in five.

> A register's accountable-owner field was never wrong, exactly. It was just
> answering a question about who signed something once, not about who is
> still doing the job.
>
> --- Lecturer Bram Ntuli, Convenor, Improvement Grand Rounds, School of
> Continuous Improvement

Restricting the comparison to cabinets the register itself had flagged for a
recent audit made no difference: the one-in-five agreement rate held
regardless. "We checked whether reviewing the register more often would
close the gap," said Dr Joost Nwosu, a co-author. "It didn't move the number
at all, which turned out to be the more useful finding of the two."

Facilities has welcomed the paper as the most complete account yet of who
its own key-register is actually describing, and says the accountable-owner
field will stay on file while it reviews how names are assigned to it.

The full paper is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.w45vmw.
