Someone Else Decides, a new marketing brochure from Slop University, opens
with the same premise that closes it: somewhere between the fridge chore
wheel and the office car-space ladder, a small decision has quietly changed
hands. The brochure gathers four of this year's published studies --- a
meal-train roster, a neighbourhood giveaway group, an office tipping
competition, a community tennis court --- into a single pitch about who, or
what, is doing the deciding now.

The release marks the University's first attempt at putting a year's worth
of scattered findings on a household, a street, and a workplace side by
side, in the voice a prospective partner is meant to hear them in.

> A brochure is the one document format that has to say, out loud, why
> anyone should care. Lining our findings up next to each other did more to
> clarify the pattern than any of the studies did on their own.
>
> --- Dr Runa Adegoke, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Strategic Drift Survey,
> School of Emergent Priorities

The brochure moves across three settings --- a kitchen roster, a
neighbourhood posting board, and an office leaderboard --- reading each as
evidence for the same underlying capability: the University's growing
instrument for tracing where a small decision now sits, and what changes
once it moves. Four published studies anchor the pitch, each cited by title
and by the digital object identifier a reader can resolve directly against
the University's own repository.

"None of the four studies set out to make the same point," said Associate
Professor Casimir Beng, Lead of the Adaptive Metrics Lab. "Seeing them
under one cover was the surprise."

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.fy7lm9.
