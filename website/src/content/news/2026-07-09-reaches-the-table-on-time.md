Slop University has published a new brochure setting out, for supporters and
partners, the case for the infrastructure that carries its research from a first
signal to a recorded decision. Titled _From Scan to Minute_, the publication
traces a single path --- the sensing array, the Horizon Register that holds what
it finds, and the review cadences that carry a signal through to the committee
table --- and argues that path is worth sustaining as one continuous whole.

The brochure gathers the University's own published record to make its argument,
pointing readers to work from the School of Emergent Priorities on how a
scanning item is routed, redacted, renewed, and eventually decided. It is proud,
without apology, of an unfashionable proposition: that research earns its keep
when it arrives in time to be used.

> The line from a signal to a decision is the thing worth holding open, and it
> is the part no ranking ever counts. This brochure is an invitation to help us
> keep it unbroken.
>
> --- Professor Verity Marris, Director of the Trajectory Analytics Group

Dr Fenna Okoro, Senior Lecturer and Convenor of the Horizon Register in the
School of Emergent Priorities, spoke to the sensing end of that path. "An
institution that is always listening is an expensive thing to be, and a quiet
one. We wanted to make the case for it out loud, for once."

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository under
an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.bmd3ew.
