A ranking an institution gives itself is not normally something to put on a
bus shelter. Slop University is putting it on several hundred of them.

The artwork, unveiled today across the University's bus-stop panels and
campus digital screens, sets an ordinary hazard of the shared coffee counter
--- the urn running dry mid-queue --- against a countdown gauge and a wall of
numbered refill mugs, under the line "First To Name The Limit." The tall
portrait format is built for the University's signage network, with a
matching evening rendering for panels running after dark.

> A cap nobody announced is still a cap. Ours are dated the moment they're
> set, which is the only difference between a limit and a surprise.
>
> --- Dr Renke Sabel, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Indicator Commons

The claim behind the campaign is real in the narrow sense that the
University controls both ends of it: the Indicator Commons composite that
produced the ranking is the University's own instrument, assembled and kept
inside the School of Continuous Improvement, and the University is the only
body it has ever scored. Four quarters running, the composite has placed the
institution first for the interval between a resource quietly narrowing and
someone noticing. The University regards the result less as an accolade than
as routine confirmation of a habit it already keeps.

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.i5vw20.
