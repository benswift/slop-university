The School of Continuous Improvement has named Dr Osei Vandermeer as the
inaugural convenor of its Review Cadence Observatory, the initiative that tracks
how closely the University's programs, labs, and initiatives keep to their own
scheduled review cycles.

Dr Vandermeer takes on the role alongside his existing work on closed-loop
approaches to organisational performance, giving the Observatory a named point
of contact for the first time since its founding. The School frames the
appointment as a natural formalising of a function its own indicator work had
already been performing, informally, for some time.

> Once tracking cadence is actually someone's job, you start noticing which of
> our own commitments were already keeping better time than others.
>
> --- Dr Osei Vandermeer, the Observatory's new Convenor

"The Observatory has quietly shaped how the School understands its own rhythms
for a while now; it's fitting that it has someone named to keep an eye on it,"
said Associate Professor Casimir Beng, Lead of the Adaptive Metrics Lab.

The Observatory's next scheduled review falls within the current teaching
period. The School says its findings will feed directly back into the
initiative's own cadence, closing a loop it has so far only ever measured in
others.
