A pub-trivia circuit spent a season laminating "phones away" reminders onto
every table in its rotation, and finished that season with almost exactly
as many disputed answers as it started with. A new research poster from the
School of Emergent Priorities lays out what sixteen weeks of quizmaster logs
did, and did not, change.

> A sign like this one tells us more about what an institution wants to be
> seen doing than about what changes underneath it. That's not a criticism
> --- it's the sort of thing the School exists to sit with.
>
> --- Senior Lecturer Fenna Okoro, Convenor of the Horizon Register, School
> of Emergent Priorities

Dr Okoro and Lecturer Solveig Adeyemi tracked fifty-eight venues across one
trivia circuit for a sixteen-week season, logging quizmaster-recorded
disputes week by week as the honour sign rolled out venue by venue over the
season's first half. Disputed-answer rates held level throughout, on signed
and unsigned tables alike, while the circuit's own self-reported compliance
survey moved on a schedule of its own.

"We didn't expect the compliance survey to keep moving on its own," said Dr
Adeyemi, "and now we're curious what else in the circuit is being measured
for its own sake."

The School counts the result among its clearer service to an industry it
doesn't otherwise study, and says as much without pretending to be
surprised by it.

The full poster is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.6ag6g7.
