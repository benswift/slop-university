> A posting doesn't need anyone's authority behind it to start being obeyed
> like a ruling.
>
> --- Dr Dagny Okafor, Lecturer and Convenor of Evaluation of Evaluation

That line, delivered by one of four researchers quoted in Slop University's
newest brochure, sets the terms for the whole pamphlet: _Official, Not
Deciding_ is not concerned with whether a decision was right, only with what
changes once something official arrives to bless it.

The brochure ties together four studies already in the University's research
record --- a lobby noticeboard's postings hardening into rulings nobody can
enforce, a volunteer ground curator's fading appetite to override a
storm-radar app, a precautionary by-law rolling past its own review unread,
and a junior-sport app's nominal human sponsor rarely intervening in what it
approves. Read together, the Schools of Emergent Priorities and Continuous
Improvement treat each as the same finding wearing a different coat: an
institution's official mark is a separate event from its decision, and
rarely a corrective one.

Dr Renke Sabel, Convenor of the Indicator Commons, put the throughline more
bluntly: a sponsor's signature and a sponsor's judgement, he said, are not
the same commitment, and an automated system will happily collect only the
first.

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.2jt6cj.
