The School of Emergent Priorities keeps a standing count of its own past
decisions still doing work nobody has re-checked; its newest publication is
the first to put three of them on the same page. _The Sort That Keeps
Watching Itself_, published this week by the Office of Research Outputs,
draws the Trajectory Analytics Group, the Horizon Register, and Futures in
Committee together around a single argument: an assignment --- a seating
group, a badge requirement, a permit tier --- doesn't end when it's made.

> We didn't need a new instrument to notice this. We needed to stop treating
> the old ones as finished.
>
> --- Dr Fenna Okoro, Senior Lecturer and Convenor, Horizon Register, School
> of Emergent Priorities

Three of the University's own published studies anchor the pitch: a
week-one diagnostic seating group tracked against week-nine performance, a
workroom badge-tap requirement examined for whether it outlived the scare
that introduced it, and a parking-permit scoring tier audited line by line
against the waitlist it replaced. Each resolves to a DOI already sitting in
the University's repository, filed before the brochure that now cites it.

Dr Dagny Okafor, whose audit of the parking-permit tier is one of the three,
called the brochure's premise "the kind of thing my own program exists to
keep asking, said back to me by a marketing document," and welcomed seeing
the habit named outside a methods section for once.

The full brochure is available from the University's research repository
under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.691kp8.
