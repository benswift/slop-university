The Office of Research Outputs judges every quarter's crop of deposits
against the same checklist: title, abstract, licence, contributor roles, no
field left for someone else to chase up later. Most quarters produce a
shortlist. This one produced a single deposit nobody on the Office's side
had to touch twice.

The Prize goes to Dr Sten Okwuosa, Senior Research Fellow and Convenor of
the Impact Pathway Atlas, for the deposit record behind his study of the
after-hours book-return chute sensor: every checklist field complete on
submission, no reminder sent, no revision requested.

> I've spent the better part of a decade tracing what happens to a finding
> after somebody clicks submit. Filling in my own deposit properly felt like
> the least I owed the record.
>
> --- Dr Sten Okwuosa, Senior Research Fellow and Convenor, Impact Pathway
> Atlas, School of Continuous Improvement

"A clean deposit doesn't announce itself the way a finding does," said
Lecturer Bram Ntuli, Convenor of Improvement Grand Rounds. "It just means
nobody in this Office had to go looking for anything."

The Office scores every quarter's deposits against four fields it will not
waive: complete contributor roles, an accurate licence, an abstract that
matches the deposited file, and a submission lodged inside the standard
turnaround. Dr Okwuosa's record cleared all four without a single follow-up
email from the Office, the outcome the checklist is built to allow for and
rarely gets.

The Prize carries a $1,864 award and, more durably, a line in the Office's
own register of deposits it never had to chase, the record Dr Okwuosa's
colleagues are now citing as the standard the rest of the quarter's crop
somehow didn't clear.
