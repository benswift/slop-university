Slop University's agentic Lost Property intake tool has spent fourteen
months quietly forecasting who is coming back for what they left behind.
From today, that forecast is also the pitch: a new recruitment campaign for
the School of Emergent Priorities puts the tool's confidence on a bus
shelter.

Unveiled today under the line "We Already Know If You're Coming Back," the
artwork sets a lost-property counter mid-sort --- umbrellas bundled, bottles
boxed, a stray glove still waiting its turn --- beside an open ledger and a
clock keeping time on whatever's left. It runs across the University's
bus-stop panels and campus digital screens in matched daylight and evening
renderings built to hold their legibility after dark.

> We spent fourteen months teaching a model to guess what you'd come back
> for. It turns out the harder question was what to do with everything it
> guessed you wouldn't.
>
> --- Dr Anouk Mensah, Postdoctoral Fellow, School of Emergent Priorities

The paper underneath, "Sorted on Arrival," tracked intake across six campus
collection points: reclaim rates rose at five of the six once the
forecaster began scheduling disposal dates on arrival, and a human-review
checkpoint built to override those dates went unused often enough that
removing it changed nothing measurable. The campaign keeps the dare and
leaves the ablation for whoever scans the code.

"Tracing a finding from a paper to a bus shelter has taught the Atlas more
about attention than the finding itself did," said Dr Sten Okwuosa, Senior
Research Fellow and Convenor of the Impact Pathway Atlas.

The full advertisement is available from the University's research
repository under an open licence, doi:10.5555/slop.c4m7ow.
